to your final cut).

## Voiceover (read straight through, ~80 s)

> These characters have no scripted dialogue. They talk, remember and change their
> plans in real time — driven by an AI running on my own laptop.
>
> Ordinary game NPCs repeat the same lines on a loop. Here, every villager has memory,
> a personality and an agenda of their own. It's a reimagining of Stanford's
> *Generative Agents* paper — but with no cloud: everything runs offline on an RTX 4060.
>
> I tell just one villager that there's a party at the plaza tonight. No one else.
> And then word starts to spread: Diego hears it from Maria and passes it to Sofia;
> Sofia tells Carlos. Nobody coded that chain — it emerges from their conversations.
>
> By evening, the ones who heard show up at the plaza, right on time. Five out of five
> knew it; four learned it by word of mouth.
>
> Under the hood there's a Python brain — memory, relevance-based retrieval, reflection
> and planning — talking to Unity over WebSocket, with a turn-based simulation so a
> single GPU can run it. And it's tested.
>
> No cloud, no APIs, no per-token cost. Just my GPU, and a village that comes alive.
